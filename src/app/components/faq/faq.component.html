Frequently Asked Questions

Where is this data sourced?

All of our data is sourced directly from the City of San Diego Electronic Filing System. It is our priority to show you unbiased accurate information about campaign finance.

How often is this site updated?

Our data is pulled from the City of San Diego Electronic Filing System weekly to ensure you are able to see the latest campaign finance data for local elections.

Who manages this site?

This site was developed in 2018 as a collaboration between the Voters' Voice Initiatives, a non-partisan community group advocating for election reform, and Open San Diego, a former brigade of the national, non-partisan Code for America. It was created with the goal of empowering voters by providing clear data on how local elections were funded.

In 2025, Represent San Diego, one of the founders of Voters' Voice, assumed sole hosting duties for the site.

In 2026, the League of Women Voters of San Diego expanded the site to include charts comparing contributions to candidates and expenditures supporting/opposing candidates, by committees not controlled by candidates.

How do I find out what city council district I am in?

Use the city's Find my Council District tool.

What's not included in the Contributions to Candidates chart?

Candidates who indicate that they do not anticipate raising or spending $2,000 or more in a calendar year (by filing FPPC Form 470) are not required to report contributions on Form 460.

Contributions totaling less than $100 per contributor per calendar year are reported in aggregate and are not itemized; accordingly, they are not included in geographical breakdowns.

What is outside money?

Outside money refers to all independent expenditures for or against a candidate.

What is an independent expenditure?

This refers to money spent on campaign communications (like ads or mailers) that is free from contribution limits because the spender operates entirely on their own, without consulting the candidate.

What is the difference between donating to a candidate's campaign committee and donating to an independent expenditure committee supporting the candidate?

Campaign contribution limits are established by municipal code and adjusted by the city clerk every two years. Current limits can be found on the San Diego City Clerk's How to Run for Office webpage. There are no limits to how much money you can donate to an independent expenditure committee working separately on behalf of, or against, a candidate.

What do the red and green spending totals under the candidate photos include?

The green "Spending in support" total is the combination of funds raised by the candidate's controlled campaign committee and outside expenditures made by independent (non-candidate controlled) committees in support of the candidate. The red "Spending in opposition" number tracks money spent by independent committees to campaign against candidates.

How does this effort help reduce the impact of money on politics?

Campaign finance disclosure is an essential public accountability mechanism. Providing public access to this kind of information about the flow of money in elections empowers oversight and accountability in the government decision-making process. Data transparency is critical to maintaining public trust.
